ACME Corporation — Employee AI Usage Policy
Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2024-01-01

1. PURPOSE
This policy governs the responsible use of AI tools, including large language models
(LLMs) and AI-powered research assistants, by ACME Corporation employees.

2. APPROVED AI TOOLS
The following AI tools are approved for internal use:
- Real-Time Research Copilot (internal tool, runs fully locally)
- Approved LLMs: Ollama-hosted models (llama3.2, gemma, mistral)
- No cloud-based LLMs may process confidential company data without written approval.

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION
Employees must not input the following into any AI tool unless explicitly approved:
- Customer personally identifiable information (PII)
- Financial projections or unreleased earnings data
- Intellectual property under NDA
- Passwords, API keys, or secrets

4. LOCAL-FIRST REQUIREMENT
All AI inference for confidential tasks must use locally hosted models via Ollama.
Cloud AI services (ChatGPT, Claude API, Gemini) are restricted for confidential tasks.

5. PRIVATE KNOWLEDGE BASE
The Research Copilot's private knowledge base may contain:
- Internal policy documents (like this file)
- Product documentation
- Approved research papers
- Meeting summaries (anonymized)

6. ACCEPTABLE USE
Approved uses of the Research Copilot:
- Summarizing internal documents
- Answering questions about company policy
- Technical research and literature review
- Drafting internal reports (requires human review before distribution)

7. PROHIBITED USE
The Research Copilot must not be used to:
- Make final decisions on hiring, firing, or compensation
- Generate customer-facing content without human review
- Replace legal or medical advice

8. AUDIT AND LOGGING
All queries submitted to the Research Copilot are logged locally for audit purposes.
Logs are retained for 90 days and reviewed monthly by the IT Security team.
